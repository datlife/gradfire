How it all started

We just wanted to take classes we would like

UC Discourse was envisioned after Andy built an open-source library to interface with the UC Davis course catalog and registration system. An architect at heart, Andy noticed that the University lacked sufficient online resources to help students navigate their degree path. In September of 2015, Andy began work with Minu on UC Discourse, automating the counseling proccess and enabling students to interact with instructors and fellow students. We launched in February of 2016 and subsequently won a grant from the ASUCD E-Fund.

Our vision

Connect the academic world and empower student curiosity

We plan to launch our new installment of UCDiscourse this October. Packed with new community features, students will be able to ask questions in an engaging environment, provide responses based on their experiences in the classroom, and build relationships with faculty who are interested in empowering the student body. Using student uploaded transcripts, we can connect students to the right individuals. So now, every student, faculty member or administrator becomes an online counselor.

After we launch and promote our new version at UC Davis, we will be gearing up for a multi-university launch at other UCs and large research institutions.
